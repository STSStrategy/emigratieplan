What if you don't run the numbers?

You carry on without the figures. Not out of laziness, but because the calculation is too complicated for a Saturday afternoon. Because it's hard to compare a calculator for Spain with one for Greece.

And then this is what happens:

You keep putting it off

You say: I'll look at it next month. Then summer comes, then the end of the year, and a year on you're still stuck with the same doubts. The plan stays in your head, but never makes it onto paper. Meanwhile you go on living a different life from the one you pictured.

You choose on gut feeling

You pick a country because friends live there, the climate is sunny, and the houses are cheaper. You carry on calculating on gut feeling. Only once you live there do you find out the tax situation is different from what you thought and that your pension turns out much lower.

You lose track entirely

You've calculated with three online tools, read ten articles, and now have four different figures in your head. But you also know that none of them is right for your situation. You can't add it all up, so you can't make a well-considered choice either.

The example behind these figures

Picture this: Hans & Esther, married, both in their mid-fifties. Together they earn €110,000 gross a year, partly in employment, partly as ZZP (sole trader). They own a home in the Netherlands with €200,000 still on the mortgage and €250,000 in savings and investments. The children are about to leave home. After that they want to emigrate to Portugal: first rent for a year, then buy. Horizon 30 years (to their mid-80s), with pension and AOW included.

The difference with the Netherlands is large: €780/month more to spend, well over four hundred thousand euros more in wealth accumulation, but a lower AOW and a more limited pension.

This is an example, not a promise. With the tool you run your own situation through the numbers.

Portugal, for instance, scores well on cashflow, tax burden and cost of living. The Netherlands scores better on pension security and social ties. The right choice depends on your horizon and your priorities.

What you compare

What we put side by side

The Netherlands is always your benchmark. We run every country on the same six points, with the same assumptions. So you compare like with like.

  • Income tax Box 1 in the Netherlands against the income tax of the other country. Including special regimes such as NHR-2, Beckham or non-dom.
  • Wealth and savings Box 3 in the Netherlands against wealth tax, dividend tax and profit tax elsewhere. Plus local levies where they apply.
  • Pension and AOW Accrual, payout and freezing of your AOW, plus private pension and annuity. With the rules from the tax treaty per country.
  • Housing Buy, rent or let out. Mortgage and interest relief, local housing costs (such as IMI, IBI or ENFIA) and the value of your home.
  • Cost of living What daily life costs per country, based on price level and purchasing power.
  • Social contributions What you pay for healthcare and social security, for employment, ZZP and BV/DGA.

A solid basis to calculate with

Where the numbers come from

The figures come from the source itself, not from blogs or estimates. We take tax rates from the tax authority of each country. For AOW (the Dutch state pension) and pensions we use the SVB (the Dutch social-security agency) and DNB (the Dutch central bank). What life costs comes from the CBS (Statistics Netherlands) and Eurostat, the EU statistics office. And the rules on which country gets to tax what are set out in the tax treaties of the Ministry of Finance and the OECD.

Compare like for like, so income tax in the Netherlands (box 1) with that of, say, Spain, Portugal or Greece. Frequently asked questions

What you'll want to know in advance

Who is Emigratieplan for?

Emigratieplan is for people who are serious about emigration and want to leave well prepared. You'll recognise yourself in the following: I want to make a well-founded choice, I know emigrating has a big impact, and I don't want to think later "if only I'd thought it through better beforehand".

What if I want to upgrade later?

Upgrading from Compare to Explore or Decide is coming in a future version. For now, you choose the package that suits how many countries you want to compare. Made the wrong choice entirely? Just email me at kevin@emigratieplan.nl and we'll sort it out.

Does this work if I'm a ZZP (sole trader)? Or in employment? Or DGA?

Yes. The tool supports all three and can also combine them: for example you in employment and your partner as ZZP. Being active together in a foreign limited company (such as IKE or Ltd) is supported too. That makes it easy to run several scenarios yourself.

Can I keep my data?

Yes! You export your scenario at the press of a button. Want to carry on later? Then you simply upload that export back into the tool. That way you can also create and save several scenarios. You don't need an account, your data stays on your device. A reassuring feeling.

Is this tax advice?

No, deliberately not. It's a calculation tool that runs scenarios through the numbers based on current rules per country. For formal advice you go to a tax adviser, for example one affiliated with NOB or RB. The tool helps you start that conversation with a concrete plan. The Decision Guide in Decide contains a personal checklist with the right questions for that conversation.

How does the guarantee work?

30 days, not satisfied = money back, no questions. Send one email to kevin@emigratieplan.nl.

What if I don't know which country yet?

Then Explore or Decide makes more sense than Compare. Explore gives you five countries plus the Netherlands on the same assumptions. Decide gives you ten plus handy tools with questions, checklists and shortlists.

Who is Emigratieplan not meant for?

Not for people leaving within three months without tax preparation. Not for people who want to avoid tax through structures without substance. Not for people who want to make the choice purely on gut feeling. It is for people who want to leave carefully, protect their wealth and pension, and see the figures before they decide.
